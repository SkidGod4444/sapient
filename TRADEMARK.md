## What you MAY do without asking

- Use the SAPIENT software under the terms of the AGPL-3.0 (or your commercial

- Fork, modify, and redistribute the **source code** under AGPL-3.0-only.
- State **factual, nominative** references — for example, "MyTool is built on
  SAPIENT", "compatible with SAPIENT", or "a fork of SAPIENT" — as long as the
  wording does **not** imply that OpenHorizon Labs produces, sponsors, or
  endorses your project, and does not use our logo as your own.
- Use the SAPIENT name in unmodified academic citations, articles, reviews, and
  tutorials that refer to the original project.

## What you MAY NOT do without written permission

- **Name your product, fork, service, app, package, domain, or company
  "SAPIENT"** or any name confusingly similar to it (including minor
  misspellings, translations, or "SAPIENT"-plus-a-suffix names).
- **White-label** SAPIENT — that is, ship it under your own brand as if it were
  your own product. This requires both a commercial license and a separate
  written branding agreement.
- Use the SAPIENT name or logo as the **primary branding** of a redistributed
  or modified version. **If you redistribute a modified version, you must
  rebrand it under your own name and remove the SAPIENT Marks.**
- Use the SAPIENT Marks in a way that suggests **affiliation, sponsorship, or
  endorsement** by OpenHorizon Labs when none exists.
- Use the SAPIENT Marks in **app-store listings, package registries
  (npm, crates.io, PyPI, Maven, etc.), or repository names** for your own

- Register or attempt to register the SAPIENT Marks, or any confusingly similar

- Modify or redraw the SAPIENT logo, or combine it with other marks.

## Redistributions and forks — the short version

If you take this repository (including downloading it as an archive), modify or
rebrand it, and publish it:

1. You **must** have a valid license for the code — either comply with
   AGPL-3.0-only (publish your complete corresponding source, including for
   network/hosted use, keep the copyright headers, `NOTICE`, and `LICENSE`, and
   state your changes) **or** hold a commercial license.
2. You **must not** call the result "SAPIENT" or present it under our logo.
   Pick your own name and branding.
3. A factual "based on / forked from SAPIENT by OpenHorizon Labs" line is
   welcome and encouraged for attribution.

Holding a code license (AGPL or commercial) does **not** grant a trademark
license, and holding a trademark does **not** override your code-license
obligations — both apply at the same time.

AGPL source disclosure or a commercial license). Unauthorized use may result in
a request to rename or take down the material, a formal notice, a platform
