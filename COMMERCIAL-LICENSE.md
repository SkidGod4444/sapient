## Do I need a commercial license?

The AGPL-3.0 is **strong copyleft**. In broad terms, if you distribute SAPIENT
(or a modified version), **or run it as a network/hosted service**, the AGPL
requires you to release the **complete corresponding source code** of your
application — including your own code that links to SAPIENT — under the
AGPL-3.0, to your users.

You most likely **need a commercial license** if you want to do any of the
following **without** open-sourcing your own product under the AGPL:

- Embed SAPIENT in a **closed-source** application, SDK, or device firmware.
- Offer SAPIENT (modified or not) as part of a **hosted or SaaS product** and
  keep your service's source code private.
- **Redistribute** SAPIENT inside a proprietary product you sell or license.
- Otherwise use SAPIENT in a way that is **incompatible with the AGPL-3.0's
  source-disclosure obligations**.

You most likely **do NOT need** a commercial license if:

- You use SAPIENT internally and do not distribute it or expose it as a network
  service to third parties; or
- You are happy to release your application's complete source under the
  AGPL-3.0.

> AGPL-3.0 covers your use, consult your own legal counsel or contact us.
